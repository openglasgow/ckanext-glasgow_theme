FOCUS

Why

The objective of the project is to develop an interactive application for primary and secondary school schools (BGE levels) to allow them to examine and analyse data around CFE levels and eventually National Standardised Assessment. This will provide head teachers with both trend and benchmarking level data to monitor achievement across their school. In line with the Glasgow’s Improvement Challenge, the National Attainment Challenge and the Pupil Equity Fund there will be a significant focus on deprivation data (SIMD) within the application.

Benefits

There is no mechanism at local or national level that provides schools with the proposed level and depth of analysis for their school that will be provided by FOCUS. Whilst there is a national tool for the senior phase (Insight) there is no indication that a BGE toll will be devised nationally, hence Glasgow City Council investing money and resource to develop its own application to support primary and secondary establishments across the city (P1 to S3).

There will be a number of benefits as schools will be able to monitor their own school and benchmark themselves against comparable schools. Not only will the application be able to demonstrate outputs and added value against a number of key national and local policies (Attainment Challenge, Pupil Equity Fund and Glasgow’s Improvement Challenge) it will also support schools in the following areas:

  • Quality Reviews
  • School Improvement Plans
  • Setting Priorities for improvement
  • Inspections
  • Handbooks
  • Work with LIGs
